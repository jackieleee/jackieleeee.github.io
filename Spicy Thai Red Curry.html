    Spicy Thai Red Curry

      HURRY, you gotta make this CURRY!

    This delicious curry dish is super simple and can be made in less than 30 minutes. If you can’t eat spicy, maybe choose a milder curry paste, but the red curry paste I used was HOT! The second time I cooked this dish I had to use much less paste…. but it was still good!
    See that green thing with weird silicon like bubbles? That’s a finger lime. Yes, a lime in the size of your finger!! Finger limes are originally from Australia. It is a small fruit, around 1-6 cm long. They taste exactly like green limes but each bubble will burst as you bite into it, making it a very unique topping. Finger limes are also really high in Vitamin C! Check out the video below for an up close shot of the limes.

    SPICY RED THAI CURRY

    Time:30 mins
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:2
    Ingredients
    • 1/2 cup rice
    • 7 oz extra-firm tofu
    • 1 carrot
    • 1 scallion
    • 1 clove garlic, diced
    • 1 inch ginger, minced
    • 1/2 onion
    • 1 3/4 cups light coconut milk
    • 4 oz spinach
    • 1 tbsp red curry paste
    • 1 tbsp coconut sugar (or regular sugar)
    • 2 tsp tamarind concentrate
    • 1 bunch cilantro
    • 2 finger limes (or regular limes)
    • 2 tbsp dried mung beans
    Instructions
    1. Start by cooking the rice
    2. While the rice cooks, cut the scallion into thin slices, crush and mince the garlic, chop the ginger, cut the onion into slices and peel and slice the carrot (thinly on an angle). Roughly chop the cilantro leaves and them, and half the finger limes crosswise.
    3. Turn heat to medium-high, when hot add the garlic, ginger, onion, white pieces of scallion, tofu, and carrot into the pan. Season with salt and pepper. You can use oil or water to help cook, stirring occasionally, 2-3 mins or until lightly brown.
    4. Add the sugar, tamarind concentrate, and as much of the curry paste as you like. Cook, stirring frequently, 1-2 min, or until fragrant.
    5. Add the coconut milk (shake before open) and spinach to the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, you can add more water if it gets too thick and sauce evaporates.
    6. Turn off heat, season with salt and pepper to taste.
    7. Divide the cooked rice between 2 dishes. Top with the finished curry. Garnish with the cilantro, green top of the scallion, lime, and mung beans.
