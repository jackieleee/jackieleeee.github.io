    Almond Butter Donuts

      Donuts make me Go NUTS!

    This was my first-time ‘baking’ donuts, or even making donuts and I am pleasantly surprised. Baked donuts have a different texture compared to normal donuts and are more like sponge cakes. This recipe is very creamy and doughy. Although the donuts are small, they definitely make a decadent dessert. The glaze is perfect, but if served as dessert, you might want to consider serving it with some ice cream!
    Original recipe from Chocolate Covered Katie

    ALMOND BUTTER DONUT

    Time: 15 mins
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:6
    Ingredients
    • 1/4 cup + 2 tbsp milk of choice
    • 1 1/2 tsp white or apple cider vinegar
    • 3 1/2 tbsp almond butter or allergy-friendly sub
    • 1/3 cup pure maple syrup, honey, or agave
    • 1/4 tsp pure vanilla extract
    • 1 cup spelt, white, gf ap, or oat flour
    • 1 tsp baking powder
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1/4 cup mini chocolate chips, optional
    • Glaze:
      • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
      • 1 tbsp almond butter
      • 2 tbsp milk of choice
      • 1/8 tsp pure vanilla extract
      • Pinch of salt
    Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 350 F
    2. Soak dates in hot water for 2-3 minutes
    3. In a blender, blend together Medjool dates and coconut milk.
    4. In a bowl, whisk together all dry ingredients (flour to baking soda)
    5. Combine date paste mixture, lemon juice, and vanilla with dry ingredients
    6. Fill up donut pan with batter mixture
    7. Bake for 10 minutes and then let cool for 5 minutes
    8. In a small bowl, combine glaze mixture.
    9. After donuts have cooled, dip the donut into the glaze and let cool.
