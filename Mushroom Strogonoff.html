    Mushroom Stroganoff

      Do you love mushrooms too?

    This is the perfect dish that is neither difficult nor easy. It takes about 20 minutes to make but is pretty simple. If you love mushrooms, this is the dish for you! It’s not too creamy and not brothy, but just right. I had all of these things in my fridge already and didn’t have to go out to buy anything. Simply delicious!
    Original Recipe from One Green Planet

    MUSHROOM STROGANOFF

    Time:30 mins
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:2
    Ingredients
    • 8 ounces uncooked ribbon noodles
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 yellow onion, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons whole wheat flour, divided
    • 2 cups beefless beef broth or vegetable broth
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon tomato paste
    • 1 1/2 pounds mushrooms (half Portobello and half White Button mushrooms), cut into large 2-inch chunks
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried sage
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
    • 10 turns of fresh ground, black pepper
    • 1/4 cup flat-leaf parsley, minced
    Instructions
    1. Cook the noodles per the direction on the package. Undercook them a bit because they will be cooked again once incorporated into the sauce.
    2. Drain and set aside.
    3. In a large saucepan, add the olive oil and sauté the onions for 3 minutes over medium heat.
    4. Add the 1 tblsp flour and cook for 30 seconds, stirring constantly.
    5. Gradually add the broth, soy sauce, lemon juice, and tomato paste, while stirring at the same time. Stir until mixture becomes thick and bubbly, about a minute.
    6. Add the mushrooms, thyme, sage, and salt. Stir to combine.
    7. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring frequently until mushrooms have shrunk in size.
    8. Add the vinegar and simmer for 4 more minutes.
    9. Add the noodles, 1 tblsp of flour, black pepper, and parsley and cook on low for an additional 5 minutes.
    10. Garnish with parsley.
