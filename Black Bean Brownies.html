    Black Bean Brownies

      Don’t be afraid of the black beans!

    So I’ve wanted to try this recipe for a while as I’ve been seeing videos of people making them. I made it give to friends and told them they were regularly brownies. Everyone was surprised when I revealed that they were made from black beans! The only thing was they came out too liquidy for mine. Looked and felt like a ‘vegan raw treat’.
    They came out way mushier than they were supposed to be. Probably because I used way more maple syrup than needed. ( I substituted it for sugar as well). They were good, but I will definitely try to remake them to perfect it. They came out way too sweet anyway so maybe next time I will just omit the extra sugar and cut down a little bit on the maple syrup as a whole. I folded in the chocolate chips but forgot to top them after they came out. I think that would’ve made a big difference, but next time I will be sure to!
    This recipe was from Chocolate Covered Katie

    BLACK BEAN BROWNIES

    Time: 30 mins
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:16 (or 12 bigger pieces)
    Ingredients
    • 1 15-oz can black beans (drained and rinsed) (250g after draining)
    • 2 tbsp cocoa powder (10g)
    • 1/2 cup quick oats (40g)
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1/3 cup maple syrup (or agave)
    • pinch uncut stevia or 2 tbsp sugar (or omit and increase maple syrup to 1/2 cup)
    • 1/4 cup coconut oil
    • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
    • 1/2 tsp baking powder
    • 1/2 to 1/3 cup chocolate chips
    Instructions
    1. Preheat oven to 350 F.
    2. Combine all ingredients, EXCEPT the chocolate chips, in a food processor and blend until smooth.
    3. When completely smooth, fold in chocolate chips.
    4. Pour into a greased 8×8 pan. Top with extra chocolate chips. Bake for 15-18 min, then let cool before cutting.
