    French Bread

      Want to impress your friends at wine night?

    This recipe makes French bread that is moist on the inside and crispy on the outside! SO much better than store-bought bread and goes well with anything! I made three loaves and ate two in one night. Not only are they delicious, but they look so cute! Yes, it takes a few hours, but it’s not much work – most of it is just waiting around and letting it sit so that the flour can rise.
    Original recipe from Mary’s Test Kitchen

    FRENCH BREAD

    Time: 4-5 hours
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:2 big loaves or 3 mini loaves
    Ingredients
    • 1 cup warm water
    • 2 1/4 teaspoons traditional or instant yeast
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose white flour (plus more for kneading)
    Instructions
    1. Add yeast to water and let bloom for 10 mins
    2. Add a half cup of flour and the salt to the yeast mixture and whisk together
    3. Add 2 more cups of flour and stir together until the dough comes together (i eventually had to use more water because it got too dry)
    4. Knead the dough into a ball
    5. Cover the dough in a bowl and let rest for 15 – this will make it easier to knead
    6. Knead for 10 mins
    7. Cover and let rise for 1 hour
    8. Punch down the dough and knead again for a few mins
    9. Place the dough back back into the bowl to let rise for an hour again
    10. Punch the dough down and divide the dough into equal pieces
    11. Place them on a baking pan lined with parchment paper
    12. Cover the loaves with a damp, wet napkin and let rise for 45 mins (the dough will double in size) Preheat the oven to 450 F (232 C)
    13. Place a baking pan on the bottom rack in your oven. Place the second rack in the middle of the oven.
    14. When the oven is hot, remove the napkin from the dough
    15. Make slashes on the top of the loaves
    16. When the oven is well-heated, place the loaves on the middle rack and quickly pour the cup of water in the baking pan below and close the oven door (Watch out for the steam)
    17. Bake for 25 minutes
    18. Remove the loaves from the oven and cool them on a wire rack
    19. Wait at least a half hour before slicing.
