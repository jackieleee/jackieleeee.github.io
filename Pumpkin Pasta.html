    Pumpkin Pasta

      It’s Fall, pumpkins are everywhere!!!

    Of course, I had to try the pumpkin pasta! I didn’t get to find sage when making this when I took pictures of it, but it is definitely an important ingredient to have. It adds a lot of flavor to the pumpkin. This recipe is super simple, as I only used canned pumpkin puree. If you want to puree the pumpkin yourself, that might even be better. But this was super fast and easy to whip up. I put a twist on the original recipe and added carrots and cauliflower to get in some extra veggies that I had in the fridge.   When I cook pasta, I normally like a mix of everything. A mix of chickpea pasta, red lentil pasta, and brown rice pasta. I find that this way, I get more texture.
    Original recipe from the Viet Vegan

    PUMPKIN PASTA

    Time:30 mins
    Difficulty: Medium
    Servings:1
    Ingredients
    • 1 lb penne or other short pasta (I used a mix of different short pastas: lentil sedanini, chickpea penne, brown rice fusilli)
    • 1/2 Tbsp vegan butter
    • Small handful of fresh sage
    • 1/4 yellow onion, chopped
    • 1 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/2 can (14 oz) pumpkin purée
    • 1 carrot
    • 1/4 head cauliflower
    • 3/4 cups vegetable broth
    • 1/4 cup full-fat coconut milk
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1/4 tsp black pepper
    • 1/8 tsp ground nutmeg
    Instructions
    1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions and drain.
    2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter and fry the sage until darkened and edges look crispy. Remove from pan and let drain to crisp up on a paper towel.
    3. In the same saucepan, add the onion and garlic and sauté until the onion has softened and is beginning to brown.
    4. Stir in all the remaining ingredients and bring to a simmer. Add the pasta to the sauce. Toss well to coat, and serve with the fried sage on top!
